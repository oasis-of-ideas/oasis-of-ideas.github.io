FAQ

1What is Oasis?

Oasis is an online platform where people with spare project ideas can share them with the world, so that other people (who are looking for project ideas) can pick them up and work on them.

2 What do you mean by project ideas?

A project idea is an initial concept or proposal that outlines the framework for a potential project. It is typically specific, detailed and actionable. Importantly, it’s just a good starting point for thinking; there’s always scope for fine-tuning.

For example, “help improve knowledge retention” wouldn’t really count as a project idea, but “create a software that periodically generates summaries of the content you saved on Instagram to help you retain more content” likely would. Of course, even the latter would benefit from a more detailed description.

3What kind of project ideas do you accept?

All kinds! We welcome project ideas about research, businesses, non-profit organisations, software products, chrome extensions, video games, physical products, and more. When you submit an idea, you’ll get the option to select which areas it belongs to.

4 Why should you contribute your project ideas?

By sharing your idea on Oasis, you increase the chance that it will actually be brought to life. This leads to a multitude of benefits, both personal and societal:

  • Direct personal benefit: The executed idea may specifically address issues that you face in your own life. This is especially true for product ideas, where you yourself could be a potential customer, and for research ideas, where you might be curious about the answer to the research question.
  • Emotional fulfillment: You may not realise it, but seeing a concept of yours become reality is incredibly emotionally fulfilling.
  • Intellectual Stimulation: Oasis is a hub for creative minds. Posting your idea opens the door to engaging discussions, allowing you to delve deeper into its strengths and weaknesses. For many, this intellectual discourse is a reward in itself.
  • Contribute to societal progress: Your ideas are great starting points for projects that benefit humanity. Your contributions enrich the collective pool of ideas that can be used to solve real-world problems.
5 Why should you look for project ideas on Oasis?

Oasis is the optimal platform for discovering project ideas for several reasons:

  • Focused Content: We focus solely on project ideas, eliminating the need for you to wade through all the irrelevant content that one often encounters on more generalized social media platforms.
  • Dynamic Idea Pool: Unlike static blogs that offer a limited set of ideas, Oasis is a constantly evolving platform. You'll find a continuous influx of fresh, innovative concepts to choose from.
  • Precision Matching: Through our filtering systems, you can choose to only see ideas that cater to your specific interests and capabilities.
  • Quickly Sense Engagement: Not only do you quickly find ideas on Oasis, you also quickly find out how interested the community is in that idea and what their main concerns (if any) are. If you got an idea from Oasis, you’re already a step ahead in determining the feasibility of the idea.
  • Easily Find Collaborators: Press a button to suggest that you're interested in working on a project. Then see who else is interested and collaborate!

6 How much do you charge?

Nothing! It’s all free. Our aim is to facilitate the sharing and discovery of project ideas without financial barriers.

While we do have plans for generating revenue in the future, they don't involve putting ideas behind a paywall, as that contradicts the very essence of our platform.

7 When does Oasis go live?

The prototype for this repository is ready and we hope to start onboarding our first users very soon!

8 Who’s behind the scenes? Why are we doing this?

We’re a small group of students at Cornell University. We often find ourselves coming up with interesting project ideas, but we have way too many of them to pursue on our own. We’re building this platform to help people like us share such ideas so others can pick them up and the ideas can actually get executed.
